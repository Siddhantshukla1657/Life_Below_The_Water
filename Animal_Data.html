Description Box

Axolotls, often called the Mexican walking fish, are not actually fish but neotenic salamanders known for retaining their larval features throughout life. They possess a unique, endearing appearance marked by feathery external gills, a wide head, and a perpetual smile.

Native to the freshwater lakes and canals of Xochimilco near Mexico City, these aquatic creatures have evolved to live their entire lives underwater. In the wild, axolotls feed on small fish, worms, and insects, while in captivity, their diet is carefully supplemented with pellets and live prey.

Perhaps the most remarkable feature of the axolotl is its extraordinary regenerative ability. They can regrow lost limbs, parts of their spinal cord, and even portions of their heart and brain. This ability has made them a key model organism in scientific research on tissue regeneration and potential medical breakthroughs.

Despite their fascinating biology, axolotls are critically endangered in the wild due to habitat destruction, water pollution, and the invasion of non-native species. Conservation efforts, including habitat restoration and captive breeding, are crucial to ensure the survival of this unique species.
